Frequently Asked Questions

How often should my horse's teeth be floated? +

Generally, we recommend an annual dental exam for adult horses. Young horses (under 5) and senior horses (over 15) may require examinations every 6 months as their teeth change more rapidly.

What constitutes a veterinary emergency? +

Signs of colic (pawing, rolling, looking at flanks), severe lacerations, sudden non-weight-bearing lameness, eye injuries, and difficulty giving birth (dystocia) are all absolute emergencies. When in doubt, always call.

Do you offer payment plans? +

Payment is expected at the time of service. However, we do accept CareCredit and work with major equine insurance providers. Please discuss this with our office staff prior to your appointment.

Pharmacy & Prescription Policies

Guidelines regarding mobile drug inventory refills and script coordination rules.

⏳ Refill Processing Timelines

Prescription refills requested via the client portal require up to 48 hours for clinical authorization and compounding. Hand-delivery is completed on your next scheduled route day.

🩺 Required VCPR (Vet-Client-Patient Relationship)

Under state law, a veterinarian must have examined your animal or herd within the past 12 months to establish a valid VCPR before issuing or refilling any prescription medications.
